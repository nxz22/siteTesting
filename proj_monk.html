Overview

Created a playful and technically complex system allowing a monkey to "play" Pokémon Emerald through movement-based controls. Modified the game to replace a Pokémon with a custom-designed monkey sprite. Developed a machine learning algorithm trained on a dataset of over 2000+ monkey images to accurately detect the monkey's position.

Project Highlights

Monkeys playing video games would be funny.

The inspiration for this project stemmed from a desire to explore the potential of integrating animals with video games in a humorous way. The challenge was to create a system that could accurately interpret the monkey's movements and translate them into in-game actions. This required a comprehensive understanding of both game modding and machine learning. Modding knowledge was acquired through the decompilation of the game, learning from the open-source project found on GitHub.

Initially, I just doodled a few sketches of the monkey, driven to enhance immersion. It felt weird having monkeys control gameplay without being a part of it. This led me to delve into modding and customizing the game's Pokémon data, replacing Aipom's data with my own creation. A few key takeaways included the 15-color limit for each sprite and the need to consider factors like the game's HUD and a 32x32 pixel display. This constraint required thoughtful design to make the monkey visually appealing within these limits.

The program's functionality relies on an XML file, compiled from a comprehensive database of over 2000+ monkey images. To synchronize gameplay with real-time monkey movements, I employed a unique approach of monitoring for battle state via memory reading from 'pokeemerald' data structures. The visual design of the program's interface features a simple yet effective layout: a video feed of the monkey divided into four quadrants by red lines. Each quadrant is labeled with an attack option in the bottom left corner, corresponding to manually inputted game attacks. This setup not only enhances user interaction but also creates an immersive experience, simulating a collaborative play with a real monkey.

This project as an assignment was focused on collaboration and innovation. It reinforced the idea that combining diverse expertise leads to more comprehensive solutions. Through the fusion of hardware engineering, software development, and user experience design, we crafted a system that not only secures data but also engages the user in a unique way.

The experience taught me the importance of flexibility and adaptability in problem-solving. Adapting the accelerometer's capabilities to a simple application, we explored our own ideas of how to define security systems. This endeavor highlighted how stepping out of conventional boundaries can lead to groundbreaking innovations.

Realizing this project would not have been as successful on my own, it was the diverse ideas and perspectives around me that enabled us to approach and achieve our shared desired outcome. In summary, this project was more than just an academic exercise; it was a real-world application of innovative teamwork. It has left me with a deeper understanding of the harmony between different technological fields and a renewed enthusiasm for creative problem-solving.
